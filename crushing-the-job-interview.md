## The Framework: STAR Method Revisited

As mentioned in our resume guide, the **STAR** method is your best friend during behavioral interviews.

* **S**ituation: Set the scene.
* **T**ask: What was the challenge?
* **A**ction: What did *you* specifically do?
* **R**esult: What was the outcome? (Use numbers!)

## Top 3 Interview Questions (and How to Ace Them)

### 1. "Tell me about yourself."
This is usually the icebreaker. Do not recite your life story.
* **The Strategy:** Use the "Present, Past, Future" formula. Talk about your current role, briefly touch on relevant past experience, and finish with why you are excited about *this* future opportunity.

### 2. "What is your greatest weakness?"
Avoid humble-brags like "I work too hard."
* **The Strategy:** Be genuine but strategic. Pick a real weakness, but immediately follow up with the steps you are taking to improve it.
    * *Example:* "I sometimes struggle with public speaking, so I recently joined a Toastmasters club to improve my confidence."

### 3. "Why do you want to work here?"
They want to know if you've done your homework.
* **The Strategy:** Connect your personal values with the company's mission. Mention specific projects, culture, or recent news articles about them.

## Virtual Interviews: The New Standard

Remote interviews require specific preparation:

1.  **Test Tech:** Check your camera and mic 15 minutes early.
2.  **Lighting:** Ensure the light source is in front of you, not behind you.
3.  **Eye Contact:** Look at the *camera lens*, not the screen, when speaking.

## Questions YOU Should Ask

Never say "I have no questions" at the end of an interview. It shows a lack of engagement. Instead, ask:

* "How does this team measure success?"
* "What is the biggest challenge facing the department right now?"
* "Can you describe the company culture in three words?"

## Conclusion

Confidence comes from preparation. Practice your stories, research the company, and breathe. You have got this.
